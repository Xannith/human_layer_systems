How engagements work

Scoping conversation

A 30-minute call to map the problem, understand organizational context, and determine whether there is a fit. No commitment required. Most organizations leave with a clearer picture of the problem regardless of whether we proceed.

Proposal & scope definition

If there is a fit, we produce a written scope document that defines the engagement: objectives, deliverables, timeline, working rhythm, and what we will need from your team. Scope is fixed, not open-ended.

Engagement execution

Work proceeds in defined phases with regular check-ins, visible progress, and early course-correction if priorities shift. We work at the level of your organizational leadership, not as a downstream resource.

Deliverable and handoff

Engagements close with clear deliverables, documentation of decisions and rationale, and a handoff designed to be self-sufficient. Your team should not need us to maintain what we built.

Not sure which engagement fits?

A 30-minute scoping call is usually enough to identify the right starting point. There is no obligation to proceed, and you will leave with a clearer picture of the problem regardless.
