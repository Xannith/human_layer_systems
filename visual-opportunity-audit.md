## Executive Summary

Human Layer Systems is a site built around several sophisticated, interconnected concepts — epistemic debt, AI fluency vs. AI literacy, the human layer, stateless knowledge architecture, the Crawl/Walk/Run/Fly model — that would all benefit from visual explanation. The site's copy is tight and confident, but the concepts ask readers to hold multiple abstract ideas in their heads at once without any visual scaffolding.

The most critical gap is the absence of a **visual language for the core frameworks**. The Crawl/Walk/Run/Fly model, the dual-layer architecture, the governance hierarchy, and the AI literacy/fluency distinction are each described well in text but never shown. These are the exact moments where a reader either grasps the differentiation and stays — or doesn't and bounces.

The **SKA whitepaper** (`knowledge.html`) is a 29-section academic document with zero diagrams, despite describing multiple architectural systems that are inherently visual. It is the highest-density visual opportunity on the site.

The **case studies** contain impressive quantitative outcomes (80%+ retention, 40% onboarding reduction, 25% delivery improvement) that are completely buried in three-column prose. Visual stat callouts would be among the quickest wins for credibility and conversion.

**Sage the octopus** exists as a mascot with two images and a video clip but has no systematic role anywhere on the site except the About page. There is enormous untapped potential to use Sage as a visual anchor across teaching moments, concept introductions, and callout graphics.

## Recommended Reusable Visual Systems

### System 1: The HLS Framework Visual Language
A family of diagrams that explain the three core HLS frameworks in consistent visual style:
- The Crawl/Walk/Run/Fly Fluency Ladder (ladder or arc shape)
- The Dual-Layer Architecture (two-tier diagram with clear layer separation)
- The Knowledge Governance Hierarchy (pyramid or layered triangle)
- The Epistemic Debt Timeline (accumulation curve with intervention point)

All built on the same design grid, color palette, and typographic system. Each works standalone on a page, as a social graphic, or as a pitch deck slide.

### System 2: The Sage Contextual Visual Family
Multiple versions of Sage the octopus in defined teaching/illustrative contexts:
- Sage Guiding (next to framework diagrams and callouts)
- Sage Reviewing (near verification and human-in-the-loop themes)
- Sage Pointing (at stats, data, or key claims)
- Sage at Work (in flow diagrams representing the human layer in practice)

Sage should appear at every point where a conceptual leap is asked of the reader — specifically on the homepage framework section, the SKA whitepaper, and the AI fluency programs page.

### System 3: Branded Stat and Outcome Cards
A consistent visual template for displaying quantitative outcomes:
- Stat cards (number + label + supporting sentence)
- Case study "before/after" panels
- Testimonial quote cards with attribution

These work on the homepage trust bar, case studies, and as standalone LinkedIn/social shareable assets.

### System 4: Service Tier Icon Set
Distinct icons for each HLS offering (Clarity Session, Spark Solo, The Spark, Sprint, Steady, Knowledge Architecture Engagement). The current site uses Unicode symbols (◽, ◾, ◉). Branded icons would create visual differentiation and scanability across service listing pages.

### System 5: Process Step Visuals
A consistent visual grammar for multi-step processes:
- Numbered phase timelines (horizontal or vertical)
- Flowcharts for decision-path content (e.g., "which program fits you")
- Migration/transformation pipeline diagrams (specifically for SKA section 15)
