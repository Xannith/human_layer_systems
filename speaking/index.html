Talk topics

The Future of Enterprise Knowledge Systems

Why most organizations are unprepared for AI adoption — and why the problem is not the AI. This talk examines what redesigning information architecture actually requires, what the common failure modes are, and what organizations that get it right do differently. Audience: enterprise leaders, heads of knowledge, operations, and AI teams.

From Documentation Chaos to Scalable Knowledge

A practical framework for moving legacy content and fragmented documentation into structured, AI-ready systems without losing institutional history. Based on real transformation work at enterprise scale. Audience: knowledge managers, content strategists, digital transformation leaders.

AI Fluency Starts With Content Architecture

Why enterprise AI tools underperform — and why fixing the model is the wrong response. This talk makes the case that AI fluency is fundamentally a content architecture problem, explains what that means in practice, and provides a clear framework for addressing it. Audience: AI teams, technology leaders, operations executives.

Stateless Content Design for the Modern Enterprise

How modular, context-independent content architecture eliminates duplication, reduces maintenance burden, and improves retrieval accuracy for both humans and machines. An introduction to the principles behind Stateless Knowledge Architecture. Audience: content architects, technical writers, knowledge engineers.

Knowledge Governance Without the Bureaucracy

Most content governance fails because it creates more process than it prevents risk. This talk presents a governance model designed for operational reality: clear ownership, lightweight oversight, and quality standards that self-enforce through architecture rather than policy. Audience: operations leaders, compliance teams, content owners.
